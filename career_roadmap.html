Doctor (MBBS) Roadmap

Complete step-by-step guide to becoming a doctor in India

Career Roadmap

1

Class 11-12 (Science - Biology)

Take PCB (Physics, Chemistry, Biology) with English. Aim for at least 50% in PCB (40% for reserved categories).

Key Subjects
Physics, Chemistry, Biology, English
Important Exams
NEET-UG (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test)
2

Crack NEET-UG

Qualify NEET with high rank to get admission in top medical colleges. Minimum 50th percentile for general category.

Eligibility
17 years old, 12th pass with PCB
Exam Pattern
180 questions, 3 hours, 720 marks
3

MBBS (5.5 years)

Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery - 4.5 years academic + 1 year compulsory internship

Phase I (1.5 years)
Pre-clinical: Anatomy, Physiology, Biochemistry
Phase II (1.5 years)
Para-clinical: Pathology, Pharmacology, Microbiology
Phase III (1.5 years)
Clinical: Medicine, Surgery, Pediatrics, OBG
4

Internship (1 year)

Rotatory internship in various departments - Medicine, Surgery, Pediatrics, OBG, Ortho, etc.

5

Registration with MCI

Register with Medical Council of India or State Medical Council to practice as a doctor

6

Post-graduation (Optional)

MD/MS in various specializations - 3 years duration. Appear for NEET-PG.

Clinical
MD in Medicine, Pediatrics, Radiology
Surgical
MS in Surgery, Ortho, ENT, Ophthalmology
7

Super-specialization (DM/MCh)

Further specialization - 3 years. For example, Cardiology, Neurology, Urology, etc.

Quick Info

  • Duration: 5.5 years (MBBS) + 3 years (PG)
  • Entrance Exams: NEET-UG, NEET-PG
  • Average Fees: ₹5-20 Lakhs (Government), ₹30-80 Lakhs (Private)
  • Starting Salary: ₹6-15 LPA (Junior Doctor)

Top Medical Colleges

  • AIIMS, New Delhi
  • Christian Medical College, Vellore
  • Armed Forces Medical College, Pune
  • JIPMER, Puducherry
  • KEM Hospital, Mumbai

Key Skills Required

Empathy Patience Attention to Detail Communication Problem Solving Teamwork Stress Management
